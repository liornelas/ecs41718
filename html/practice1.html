Rosa Parks

The Mother of the Civil Rights Movement

Rosa Parks was an activist in the Civil Rights Movement who refused to give up her seat on a bus to make room for white passengers when she was instructed to, and was then arrested.
She then started a bus boycott to fight and change the rules in order to have equal rights on the buses and other public facilities.
Because of her arrest, and 381 days of the boycott, the buses were desegregated.


    1. In 1999 Parks was prresented with the Congressional Gold Medal
    2. Time Magazine named Rosa Parks as one of the 20 most powerful and international figures of the century
    3. Wrote a book titled Rosa Parks: My Story, which reflected on how she felt during the bus experience
    4. In 1983 she was inducted into the Michigan's Hall of Fame
    5. Rosa Parks was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1996
